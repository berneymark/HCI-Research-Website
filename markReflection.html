Our research assignment was focusing on the usability of a stylus: - we decided to use the Wacom Intuos Pen and Pad as our focus stylus as Wacom is a well-known and respected brand in the stylus world. However, we recognise different stylus’ will have different experiences. Each member of our team tested three users coming to a total of nine users worth of results.

Only 4:9 participants enabled palm rejection showing that the switch may be a little bit too difficult to find, being a small black switch on the back of the pad. However, only one participant had any issues with setting up, possibly due to lack of technical understanding. This shows us that despite the palm-rejection toggle being difficult, the overall setup of everything else was not too difficult for the average user to get.

The average amount of mistakes made was 6.22 in the maze, with the most being 15 and least being 0. The user who made 15 mistakes was the only one who could not set up the pad so considering that is an outlier, I would consider the stylus to be very accurate, but obviously to perform better for users who are technically familiar, especially those who have used stylus’ before. The signature comparison reaches a similar conclusion.

Users we interviewed seemed to find it reasonably comfortable with each user having something they may like to change. Many requests were for a rubber grip, while most users were happy with the provided space on the pad and the weight of the stylus. Users rated the hold as 3, 4 or 5 while writing was a little lower at 2, 3 or 4. This insinuates that while it was fine to hold it may not have been as comfortable for writing over a period of time. Every participant except one rated the feeling of the hold above that of the feeling of writing.
